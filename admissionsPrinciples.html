Admission Principles of SDEC

  • Admissions Principles

    • Admissions Principles

    Admission to SDEC International School is determined by many factors. Prospective students are expected to show a strong learning ethic, a track record of appropriate school behavior, and a genuine desire to be part of SDEC International School. Teachers play a crucial role in supporting students to become confident, independent learners, and we anticipate full parental support and involvement in their children’s education.
    SDEC International School places emphasis on understanding and supporting its mission to cater to a diverse student population, encompassing various racial, ethnic, cultural, and socioeconomic backgrounds. The school community, including administrators, teachers, students, and parents, collaborates to uphold an inclusive learning environment.

    Admission decisions are based on a combination of factors, including academic evaluations,
    English proficiency, prior school records, student interviews and classroom observations for Early Years. As for Primary and Secondary students, English and Mathematics entrance tests are administered. The academic year spans from June to March for Year 2 to Year 9 and from June to May for Nursery to Year 1 and Year 10 to Year 13..The age of entry is determined referenced to the chart in our age guidelines section.
    The decision regarding admissions is made by the School’s Board of Academic Directors, and the admission policy is designed to ensure a systematic and efficient process for admitting students to the school.
